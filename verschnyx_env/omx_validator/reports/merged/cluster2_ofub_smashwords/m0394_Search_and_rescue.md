# Search and rescue

></a>Search and rescue</h3><p>The Turkish state-owned <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anadolu_Agency">Anadolu Agency</a> showed the initial video footage of the aircraft crashing and the two pilots parachuting down. Russian helicopters conducted low-altitude <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Combat_search_and_rescue">combat search and rescue</a> flights in the area to look for the pilot.<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-press-73">[72]</a> As the Russian armed forces began planning an operation to extract the Su-24 pilot, Iranian General <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qasem_Soleimani">Qasem Soleimani</a> contacted them and proposed that a special task force unit be formed of Hezbollah's special forces and Syrian commandos who had been trained by Iran and were familiar with the geography of the region to be tasked with the ground operations for the pilot's rescue, with Russia providing transport, logistical support, air cover and satellite intelligence. The team of 26 special forces commandos which General Soleimani put forth consisted of 8 Hezbollah special forces personnel and 18 Syrian special forces commandos.<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-timesofisrael_com-74">[73]</a><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-heraldscotland_com-75">[74]</a></p><p>Two <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mil_Mi-8">Mil Mi-8</a> helicopters were sent to find and recover the pilots from the crash site. One of the helicopters was damaged by small-arms fire from <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrian_Turkmen_Brigades">Syrian Turkmen Brigade</a> militants, resulting in the death of a <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naval_Infantry_%28Russia%29">naval infantryman</a>, and was forced to make an <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emergency_landing">emergency landing</a>.<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-76">[75]</a><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-nyt2-77">[76]</a> All the surviving crew of the helicopter were rescued and evacuated later on.<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-rt_com-78">[77]</a> The <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_Syrian_Army">Free Syrian Army</a>'s <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1st_Coastal_Division">1st Coastal Division</a> claimed that they subsequently destroyed the abandoned helicopter using a US-made <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BGM-71_TOW">BGM-71 TOW</a> missile.<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-79">[78]</a></p><p>The body of the dead pilot was handed over to Turkey, which handed it on to Russian officials in Ankara to be flown to Moscow.<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-80">[79]</a><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-81">[80]</a></p><p><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown"></a>
