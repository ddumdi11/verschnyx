# Casualties

></a>Casualties</h2><p>Both pilots ejected after the aircraft was hit.<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-Buzzfeed-49">[48]</a> Initially, the deputy commander of a Turkmen <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrian_opposition">Syrian opposition</a> rebel brigade in Syria, claimed his forces had <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Attacks_on_parachutists">shot dead the two pilots</a> as they descended with parachutes,<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-61">[60]</a> and in a film a person could be heard in the background repeatedly shouting "Stop shooting!" in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_language">Turkish</a> as the parachutists descended.<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-62">[61]</a> A Turkish official reported that he believed both were alive.<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-IBT-63">[62]</a> It was also reported that one of the pilots had been captured by the Turkmen fighters and a video was circulated which allegedly showed the pilot's body.<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-64">[63]</a></p><p>The deputy commander of a Turkmen rebel brigade in Syria was later identified as Alparslan Çelik, a Turkish citizen.<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-65">[64]</a> In Turkey in 2014 it had been reported that his father, the former municipal mayor in the ciy of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elaz%C4%B1%C4%9F">Elazığ</a> and ultra-nationalist <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nationalist_Movement_Party">Nationalist Movement Party</a> (MHP) member Ramazan Celik, was proud his son was fighting in Iraq.<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-66">[65]</a></p><p>Russia's military general staff spokesman Lieutenant General Sergei Rudskoi confirmed that one pilot, Lieutenant Colonel Oleg Anatolyevich Peshkov, was killed by ground fire;<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-67">[66]</a><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-68">[67]</a> the other pilot, who was the navigator, was rescued.<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-weapon-1">[1]</a><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-69">[68]</a></p><p>The shooting of an ejecting aircraft pilot is in contravention of the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geneva_Convention">Geneva Convention</a>'s Article 42.<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-70">[69]</a> <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RT_%28TV_network%29">RT</a> journalist Roman Kosarev, who himself was under attack on the same day in <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latakia">Latakia</a>, was the first to interview the rescued pilot.<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-71">[70]</a></p><p>The pilot of the Su-24, Oleg Peshkov, 45, was posthumously awarded the title of <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hero_of_the_Russian_Federation">Hero of the Russian Federation</a>; the surviving weapon systems officer, Konstantin Murakhtin, and the killed rescuer, Alexander Pozynych, received the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Order_of_Courage">Order of Courage</a>.<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_Russian_Sukhoi_Su-24_shootdown#cite_note-72">[71]</a></p><h3>
